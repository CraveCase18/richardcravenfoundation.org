Richard’s Story

Born 9/8/1967 — Passed 3/21/2023. A devoted husband, father, grandfather, hard worker, racer, and friend.

Family First

Richard graduated from Forrest Strawn Wing in May of 1985. He married his high school sweetheart, Annette (Folwell) in September of ’85 at 19, soon after graduating high school. They were married for 38 years until his passing in 2023. Together they had four children—Matt, Anthony, Samantha, and Sara—who were the center of their world. Everything Richard did was for his family, and he and Annette built a loving home for their children and grandchildren.

Work Ethic & Career

After marriage, they moved to Worth, IL, where Richard worked at his uncle’s tire shop. He later worked as a roadside mechanic, then at Ivex Specialty Paper, and All Will County Auto Wreckers. In 2011 he fulfilled a dream by purchasing CR Towing & Recovery after serving as Transportation Manager at Collision Revision. Richard’s loyalty and grit earned deep respect across the community.

Health Battles & Courage

In October 2021, Richard became critically ill with COVID pneumonia. He survived an extraordinary 167 days on a ventilator and 312 days in hospitals and rehabilitation. He made it home just before his 56th birthday and fought to rebuild. With his parents Kathy and Wayne by his side, he regained speech and steps. Complications from pneumonia ultimately led to his passing on March 21, 2023.

His Legacy Lives On

Richard poured his heart into his wife, children, and grandchildren—always giving, always showing up. The Richard Craven Foundation carries that forward—helping families with groceries, clothing, essentials, and support.
